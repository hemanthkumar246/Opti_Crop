About the project

Opti Crop Agricultural Optimisation

The main purpose of OptiCrop is to develop an advanced software system that utilizes data-driven insights to optimize agricultural production for different crops, so farmers spend less on trial and error and more on growing.

Why it matters

Farmers often struggle to determine the most suitable crop based on soil nutrients and climate conditions, which can lead to financial losses and reduced yield. OptiCrop integrates Nitrogen (N), Phosphorous (P) and Potassium (K) levels with temperature, humidity, pH and rainfall to recommend the crop best matched to a plot of land right now — not the crop that worked somewhere else, or last year.

The same insights help agricultural researchers, policymakers and agribusinesses understand crop-environment interactions and build evidence-based, sustainable farming strategies.

How the recommendation is made

Data collection

A dataset of soil and climate readings across 22 crop types is cleaned, checked for missing values, and treated for outliers using the interquartile range method.

Pattern discovery

K-Means clustering groups similar agricultural conditions together, using the elbow method to choose the right number of clusters.

Model training

A Logistic Regression classifier is trained on the seven soil and climate features to predict the most suitable crop label.

Recommendation

The trained model powers the Find your crop page, turning a farmer's soil test into an instant, specific crop recommendation.
